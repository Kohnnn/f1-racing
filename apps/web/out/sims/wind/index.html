Wind surface

Feasible as a baked CFD viewer, not as a live in-browser solver.

The recommended app pattern is to treat CFD as precomputed data. Load a matching car mesh, then attach pressure, friction, or streamline packs on top of it. The browser should visualize the result, not solve it.

  • force coefficient logs from `postProcessing/carForceCoeffs`

  1. Confirm model license - Make sure you are allowed to publish the cleaned mesh and any derived screenshots or baked overlays on the website.
  2. Export watertight STL - Put `mcl39_clean.stl` in `pipeline/openfoam/cases/mcl39-baseline/constant/triSurface/`.
  3. Choose reference area and length - Update `areaM2` and `lengthM` in `pipeline/openfoam/config/mcl39-baseline-15ms.example.json` before trusting Cd or Cl values.
  4. Export car surface CSV - Save the solved car surface from ParaView to `pipeline/openfoam/exports/mcl39-baseline-15ms/car-surface.csv`.
  5. Finalize mesh binding - Update `triangleCount` in the overlay config after you settle the render-mesh mapping.

Integration flow

How this plugs into the site

  1. extract surface scalar or vector fields from the CFD source
  2. normalize them into browser-safe scenario packs
  3. map the CFD surface data onto the render mesh or hotspot layer
  4. serve baked pressure, friction, and streamline packs through the CDN

Target routes: /learn/aero, /cars/current-spec, /sims/wind
